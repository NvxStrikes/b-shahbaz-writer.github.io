The Origin Story

Writing the stories I couldn't find

Hi, I'm Bisma. I'm a Pakistani author in my 20s writing gothic romance, literary fiction, and cozy fantasy — three genres that feel wildly different but all share the same DNA: characters who feel too much, worlds that breathe, and stories that reach for something true.

I started writing because the stories I desperately wanted to read didn't exist. Pakistani protagonists in English gothic fiction. South Asian voices in literary spaces that weren't defined by diaspora trauma. Cozy magic that felt genuinely warm, not performatively cute. So I started writing them myself.

"Can we normalise Pakistani writers writing in English not having to explain themselves? The story is enough. The voice is enough."

I believe that deeply. My characters don't exist to educate anyone. They exist because they have stories worth telling.

The Writing Life

Several WIPs, zero regrets

I'm a multi-project writer, which means I have several works-in-progress at any given time and I've made peace with that. Each one is in a different stage — some are first drafts, some are in revision, some are just vibes and a playlist right now. That's okay. Stories have their own timeline.

I'm currently looking for beta readers for some of my projects (Carnival, Letter Kings, Ma'aloe Refrains, Prophecy, Obsession) and I'd love to hear from readers who feel like they're made for these kinds of stories.

I also write essays on Substack about writing, literature, and the things that make us human. It's where I go to think out loud.
